▶ About Us

Kubo 12 Cohort 9

We were just student leaders from different cities, united by a shared ambition when we were paired up for a single capstone project in the KadaKareer's NUSA x KadaKamp Building Bootcamp. There were four of us, each bringing our own perspectives, stories, and dreams but as we brainstormed, one urgent, unifying thread emerged: the need for financial literacy.

Growing up in different parts of the Philippines, we saw how families and communities often struggle with budgeting, saving, dealing with debt, or even knowing where to start with financial planning. We realized that so many of the challenges people face—missed opportunities, cycles of hardship, anxiety over emergencies—are really rooted in a lack of simple, accessible knowledge about money.

We hope to turn everyday stories of challenge into stories of empowerment, helping people move from surviving to thriving—one skill, one community, one future at a time. That’s the goal we share, and the impact we’re determined to make.
